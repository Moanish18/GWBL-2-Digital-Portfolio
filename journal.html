Weeks 16–33 · Mandai Wildlife Group

This journal traces my growth from finalising an RPA bot and producing organisation-wide videos, to redesigning digital platforms, supporting innovation events and deepening my skills in AI, cloud and automation.

Monthly Reflections

These reflections summarise my key learning themes, mapped to my role as an aspiring AI & RPA Engineer and to the Guided Work-Based Learning outcomes.

June & July · From Technical Delivery to Human-Centred Work

Procurement RPA Bot · Copilot Prompt Engineering · MyProject100K · Guest Journey Mapping

Over the next two months of my internship at Mandai Wildlife Group, my role evolved significantly—from technical execution to strategic involvement and creative storytelling. I began by finalising the Procurement Department’s RPA Bot. Over several weeks, I iteratively developed the solution, conducted demos, and gathered feedback from Procurement Department, including the Head of Procurement. My goal was to automate repetitive tasks and improve cross-department workflows, mapping directly to my learning outcomes in AI agents and RPA systems deployment. Working closely with business users deepened my understanding of stakeholder expectations and reinforced my skill in managing real-world automation projects.

Beyond technical delivery, I took on prompt engineering for Microsoft Copilot 365 where I was analysing hallucinations, refining prompts for domain-specific queries such as Mandai park pricing, and exploring Microsoft add-ins to enhance staff productivity. These tasks highlighted gaps in my understanding of LLM limitations and data governance; I closed them through self-led research, supervisor consultations and iterative prompt testing. In parallel, I produced video content for the MyProject100K Award Ceremony and mapped the guest journey on MIRO using Design Thinking. Across these experiences I learned that digital transformation is not just about tools, but about empathy, clarity and collaboration.

August & September · Strategic Projects & Communication

AI Agent Prototype · AEIOU Learning Festival · TO Sharepoint Website · Cloud Learning

During August and September, my experience shifted toward a blend of strategic projects, technical experimentation and communication initiatives. I completed the final stages of the MyProject100K videos, taking responsibility for AV systems and slide cues during the dry run and actual award ceremony. The pressure of live operations taught me preparation, ownership and quick problem-solving. At the same time, I experimented with an AI Agent prototype using n8n and ChatGPT API, testing API integrations to generate weekly newsletters. By mid-August, we decided the prototype was not technically or financially viable. I learnt a valuable lesson in responsible innovation and knowing when to retire an idea.

September moved me into platform and content work: redesigning the Transformation Office website, building an AEIOU Learning Festival microsite and producing a newsletter and recap video. Feedback loops with colleagues sharpened my storytelling and UX decisions. Industry visits such as the Databricks Data & AI Expo and discussions with SIT adult learners broadened my understanding of data architecture, design thinking and lean methodologies. In parallel, I prepared for the AWS Cloud Practitioner certification and scoped a new SAP automation use case with Procurement. These months strengthened my decision-making, prioritisation and ability to connect technical solutions to real organisational needs.
